About us

A small group
making a smaller app.

Altara isn't a startup with a rocket-ship deck. It's one person, mostly, with help from friends — building the chat app we wanted for our own group, and now sharing it.

Why we built it

Our group has been on the same chat app for years. It got louder, busier, full of strangers — slowly turning into a feed instead of a hangout. Notifications from communities we forgot we joined. AI assistants in our DMs. Ads in the sidebar.

So one weekend in 2025, between exams, I started prototyping something simpler. A chat app that only does the things friends actually do — talk, share music, watch stuff together, make polls, get on a quick call. No "communities discovery". No nudges to invite people.

Six months later it had a name, a logo, and enough friends using it that we figured we should let other groups in. That's Altara. Not trying to replace anything — just trying to be the cozy version for the people you actually know.

A few things we
won't compromise on.

Promises to ourselves, in writing, before we forget.

/ 01

Friends first, not communities.

Altara is sized for groups you actually know. We won't bolt on a discover feed, an algorithm, or "trending servers." If that's what you want, you have other apps.

/ 02

Privacy isn't a feature.

End-to-end encryption is the default and the floor. Not a Pro perk, not a toggle in settings. We can't read your DMs, and we don't want to.

/ 03

No ads. No tracking pixels.

We make money from optional Pro upgrades. Your hangout is not the product. Your data isn't sold, profiled, or trained on.

/ 04

Ship things that feel good.

Soft animations. Snappy keyboard shortcuts. Sound design that doesn't annoy. The boring engineering that makes things feel right.

Want in?

We're in open beta. Grab the app, bring three friends, see how it feels.
